GEOGRAPHY

The Frozen Continent

Antarctica is Earth's southernmost continent, a vast frozen wilderness covering 14 million square kilometers. Almost entirely covered by ice sheets up to 4.8 kilometers thick, it contains 90% of the world's ice and 70% of its fresh water.

"Antarctica is a natural reserve, devoted to peace and science."

— Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty, Article 2 (1991)

Ice Sheets and Glaciers

The Antarctic ice sheet is the largest single mass of ice on Earth, containing about 26.5 million cubic kilometers of ice. Massive glaciers flow slowly from the interior toward the coast, creating dramatic ice shelves and icebergs that can be larger than countries.

Extreme Climate

Antarctica holds the record for the coldest temperature ever recorded on Earth: -89.2°C. Fierce katabatic winds can reach speeds over 300 km/h, making it the windiest continent. Despite all this ice, it's technically the world's largest desert.

Mountains and Valleys

Beneath the ice lie dramatic mountain ranges, including the Transantarctic Mountains that divide the continent. Mount Vinson rises 4,892 meters above sea level, making it Antarctica's highest peak. Hidden valleys and subglacial lakes exist beneath kilometers of ice.
