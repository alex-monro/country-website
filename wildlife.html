Life on the Ice

Antarctica's wildlife has evolved remarkable adaptations to survive in one of Earth's most extreme environments. From the depths of the Southern Ocean to the frozen continent, these creatures thrive where few others can.

Penguins

Emperor penguins endure the harshest winters on Earth, huddling together in temperatures reaching -40Â°C. These flightless birds can dive over 500 meters deep, holding their breath for up to 20 minutes while hunting fish and krill.

Seals

Weddell seals are the southernmost mammals on Earth. These skilled divers use their teeth to maintain breathing holes in the ice and can remain submerged for over an hour, navigating the dark waters beneath Antarctica's ice shelves.

Whales

Humpback whales migrate thousands of miles to Antarctica's nutrient-rich waters each summer. A single whale can consume up to 1.5 tons of krill per day, playing a vital role in the Southern Ocean's ecosystem.
